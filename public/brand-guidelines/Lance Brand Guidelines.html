01 — Brand

What Lance stands for

Lance is the command center for independent professionals — freelancers, consultants, agencies, and creatives who run their own show. The brand is confident, no-nonsense, and built to move.

01
Momentum
Lance helps people keep moving. The identity reflects that energy — bold gradients, dynamic mark, forward motion. Never slow, never heavy.
02
Clarity
Independent work is complex. Lance cuts through the noise. The visual language is clean and direct — nothing decorative for decoration's sake.
03
Confidence
The people who use Lance are running real businesses. The brand respects that — professional without being corporate, approachable without being casual.
04
Independence
Lance is built for people who do things their own way. The identity has personality — it's not a generic SaaS tool; it has a point of view.

The mark

The Lance mark is a dynamic four-armed asterisk — suggesting connection, direction, and momentum. It's the core shape in the system and appears consistently across all icon sizes.

Dynamic form
The four arms radiate from a shared center with intentional asymmetry — suggesting motion rather than static balance. It reads as a compass, a connector, and a cursor all at once.
Rounded superellipse container
Icons use a rounded rectangle with a corner radius of 49.9px on a 316.8×316.8 canvas — matching the iOS app icon specification. Always maintain this proportion.
Four defined variants
Orange White (gradient bg, white mark), Orange (dark bg, gradient mark), Purple Blue (ice bg, purple gradient mark), and Purple (purple gradient bg, ice mark — the primary app icon). Never improvise new colorways.

Icon variants

Each icon is designed for a specific context. Match the icon to its background — never mix styles arbitrarily.

05 — Voice & Tone

How Lance speaks

Lance talks like a smart, experienced peer — not a chatbot, not a corporation. Direct, warm, and always in service of helping people get things done.

Direct
Say what you mean.
No filler phrases, no passive voice. Get to the point and trust the reader to follow.
Human
Real language, real people.
Write like a person, not a press release. Contractions are fine. Warmth is expected.
Confident
No hedging.
Lance doesn't say "might help you maybe." It says "here's what you need." Trust the product.
Useful
Earn every word.
Every sentence should inform, direct, or reassure. If it doesn't — cut it.
✓ Do
"Your invoice was sent. You'll be notified when it's viewed."
Direct confirmation + clear next step. No jargon, no filler.
✗ Don't
"Congratulations! Your invoice has been successfully submitted to our system."
Avoid hollow praise, passive constructions, and corporate-speak.
✓ Do
"3 invoices need your attention."
Specific, actionable, numbers-first. The user knows what to do.
✗ Don't
"It looks like you may have some invoices that might require some action."
Avoid over-hedging. Be assertive when the data is clear.

06 — Usage Rules

What not to do

These rules protect the clarity and consistency of the brand across every touchpoint.

Don't rotate or skew
The logo always sits on the horizontal baseline. Never tilt, rotate, or distort.
Don't use at low opacity
The logo must appear at full opacity. Never use it as a watermark.
Don't use on busy backgrounds
Only place the logo on solid colors or the defined gradients.
Don't recolor the wordmark
Never apply color or gradients to the wordmark. Black or white only.
Don't stretch or squish
Always scale the logo proportionally. Never alter its aspect ratio.
Don't use off-brand backgrounds
Only use black, white, ice, lavender, or the defined gradients behind the logo.
